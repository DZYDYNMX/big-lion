Frequently Asked Questions

What should I do immediately if a pipe bursts in my house?

The absolute first thing you must do is locate your main water shut-off valve and turn it off to stop the flooding. This valve is typically located near your water meter at the street, in your garage, or on an exterior wall. Once the water is off, open your faucets to drain the remaining water in the lines, and then call our 24/7 emergency dispatch immediately.

How do I know if my underground main sewer line is collapsed or just clogged?

If multiple drains in your home are backing up simultaneously, the issue is in the main line. A simple clog can usually be cleared with professional hydro-jetting. However, if the line repeatedly clogs shortly after being cleared, or if you notice sinkholes in your yard, the pipe may have collapsed due to root intrusion. We use high-definition sewer cameras to confirm the exact cause without guessing.

Why does my toilet constantly run, and how much is it costing me?

A constantly running toilet is usually caused by a deteriorated flapper valve, an improperly adjusted float, or a faulty fill valve. While it sounds like a minor annoyance, a continuously running toilet can waste up to 200 gallons of water per day, easily adding $50 to $100+ to your monthly water bill.

What are the signs of a hidden slab leak?

Slab leaks occur when a pressurized water line running under your home's concrete foundation bursts. Key signs include: a sudden unexplained spike in your water bill, the sound of running water when all fixtures are off, unexplained warm spots on your floor, damp carpets, or low water pressure. We deploy advanced acoustic leak detection equipment to pinpoint the exact location.
