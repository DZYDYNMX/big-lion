Expert Insights

WHEN DO YOU NEED FIXTURE REPAIR OR REPLACEMENT?

Not every dripping faucet needs to be thrown out, but sometimes repairing a deeply corroded fixture costs more than installing a new one. We’ll be upfront with you about whether you need repairs or replacement. Here are the signs it’s time to call the experts:

PERSISTENT LEAKS

Even a slow drip can waste hundreds of gallons of water a month, driving up your utility bills and causing sink damage.

LOW WATER PRESSURE

If a single faucet has low pressure while the rest of the house is fine, the aerator or cartridge is likely failing.

SQUEAKING HANDLES

If the handles are difficult to turn or squeak loudly, the internal threads are wearing down and need replacement.

VISIBLE RUST OR MINERAL BUILDUP

Heavy corrosion not only looks bad but eventually eats through the metal, leading to sudden, catastrophic leaks.
